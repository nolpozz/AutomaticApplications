# Cover Letter Sample (voice reference)

The first account I ever ran was a campus club's TikTok with 300 followers and no
real plan. I spent two weeks just watching what other student orgs posted, then
started testing hooks and posting times against each other. By the end of the
month we were at 1,900 followers — but the part I cared about was finally knowing
*why* certain posts worked instead of guessing.

That's the thread through most of my marketing work: I like starting from the
audience and the numbers, then making something creative that actually moves them.
Whether it was a giveaway that grew an email list or a newsletter I rewrote to lift
open rates, I've learned I do my best work when I can see the results and adjust.
